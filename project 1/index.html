EARLY LIFE AND FAMILYü§©

Vijay was born on 22 June 1974 in Chennai to director S. A. Chandrasekhar and singer Shoba Chandrasekhar. He had a sister, Vidhya, who passed away young. Vijay studied at Fathima and Balalok schools and briefly pursued a Visual Communication degree at Loyola College before focusing on acting.

FILM CAREERüé¨

1984‚Äì2003: Debut and transition to lead roles

At the age of 10, Vijay made his acting debut in the Tamil film Vetri (1984), produced by P. S. Veerappa. He went on to appear in several films as a child actor, including Kudumbam, Vasantha Raagam, Sattam Oru Vilayaattu, and Ithu Engal Neethi. He also played a supporting role alongside Rajinikanth in Naan Sigappu Manithan (1985), directed by his father S. A. Chandrasekhar. Vijay made his debut as a lead actor at 18 with Naalaiya Theerpu (1992) and later featured in hits like Senthoorapandi, Rasigan, Deva, and Coimbatore Mappillai.
His major breakthrough came with Poove Unakkaga (1996), followed by successful films such as Love Today, Kadhalukku Mariyadhai, Nerrukku Ner, and Thullatha Manamum Thullum. He continued his rise with Kushi, Priyamaanavale, and the hat-trick successes Friends, Badri, and Shahjahan. In 2002, he starred in Thamizhan with Priyanka Chopra, followed by Youth, Bagavathi, and Vaseegara.

2003‚Äì11: Established actor

Between 2003 and 2011, Vijay established himself as a top star in Tamil cinema with a consistent run of box office successes. Starting with Thirumalai in 2003, he rose to new heights with Ghilli in 2004, which became the first Tamil film to cross ‚Çπ50 crore domestically and broke long-standing viewership records. He followed it with hits like Madhurey, Thirupaachi, Sachein, Sivakasi, and the blockbuster Pokkiri. In 2007, Vijay showcased his versatility in Azhagiya Tamil Magan, playing dual roles, which was well received overseas. He continued with commercially successful films like Kuruvi (2008) and Vettaikaaran (2009). Despite a few average performances, Vijay remained one of the highest-paid actors. In 2010, he completed his 50th film with Sura. In 2011, Kaavalan revived his critical acclaim and did well internationally, followed by the Diwali hit Velayudham, which proved his consistent mass appeal and solidified his stardom across regions.

POLITICAL CAREERüö©

In 2009, Vijay launched his fan club Vijay Makkal Iyakkam.[101][102] The organization supported the AIADMK alliance in the 2011 assembly elections.[103] The fan club organization contested in the local body elections in Tamil Nadu in October 2021, securing victory in 115 out of the 169 contested seats.[104] On 2 February 2024, Vijay announced the launch of his political party, Tamilaga Vettri Kazhagam (TVK).[105][106] Political analysts suggested that the TVK party might undergo expansion to other states of India.
[107] Vijay deemed the Citizenship Amendment Act 2019 passed by the BJP government as unacceptable as it will disrupt the social religious harmony of the nation.[108]
